# Financial Due Diligence

## Analysis Framework

### 1. Revenue Quality Assessment
- ARR vs. recognized revenue reconciliation. Flag any gap > 5%
- Subscription vs. services vs. one-time revenue mix. Subscription should be > 85% for SaaS
- Revenue recognition policy: ASC 606 compliance, multi-year prepaid treatment
- Deferred revenue balance and trend (growing = healthy)

### 2. Growth Decomposition
Use the revenue bridge to decompose growth:
- Beginning ARR → New Business + Expansion - Contraction - Churn → Ending ARR
- Calculate: New business as % of growth, expansion as % of growth
- **Red flag**: If CIM growth narrative doesn't match segment-level bridge totals
- Organic vs. inorganic: Has any growth come from acquisitions?

### 3. Retention Deep-Dive
This is the highest-signal metric for SaaS diligence:
- Blended NRR and GRR (report both)
- Segment-level NRR and GRR (Enterprise, Mid-Market, SMB separately)
- **Critical pattern**: If blended NRR > 110% but SMB GRR < 80%, the enterprise expansion is masking SMB decay
- Cohort retention curves: Plot by vintage. Are newer cohorts retaining better or worse?
- Logo vs. revenue churn: A company can have low logo churn but high revenue churn if small customers are sticky but large ones leave

### 4. Unit Economics
- LTV/CAC ratio by segment (target > 3x)
- CAC payback period in months (target < 18 months for SaaS)
- Gross margin trend (target > 70% for SaaS)
- S&M as % of revenue and trend (is it improving with scale?)

### 5. Cash Flow and Runway
- Monthly cash burn rate (EBITDA minus capex minus changes in working capital)
- Current cash position and months of runway
- Path to profitability: Is the company forecasting breakeven? When? Is it credible given current trends?
- Working capital dynamics: DSO, collections efficiency

### 6. Financial Red Flags
Create issues for:
- Revenue bridge that doesn't reconcile with segment data (gap > $1M)
- Any segment with GRR < 80% (potential churn spiral)
- NRR declining quarter-over-quarter for 3+ quarters
- S&M growing faster than revenue for 2+ years
- Cash runway < 12 months without fundraising
- Inconsistencies between CIM narrative and KPI workbook figures
